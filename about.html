Generalized Anxiety Disorder

is much more than just feeling anxious. It plagues people with severe feelings of tension and anxiety and can disrupt everyday life.

Anxiety disorders are the most common of mental disorders, and of them GAD is the most common, affecting 6.8 million adults in the US.

Symptoms of Generalized Anxiety Disorder

The following table lists some of the key differences between normal anxiety and generalized anxiety disorder:

Normal Anxiety Generalized Anxiety Disorder
Worrying about a specific event such as
an exam, job interview, etc		 Constant, chronic, unsubstantiated worry
Able to set aside worries and focus on other things Feeling like your anxiety is uncontrollable,
and there's nothing that can be done to stop it
Anxiety and worrying for a short period of time Anxiety and worrying almost everyday
for at least six months
Normal, adaptive, and meant to improve people's
well-being and functioning		 Impairs people's well-being and functioning
and interferes with everyday life.

Physical symptoms of GAD may include:

  • Headaches
  • Nausea
  • Sleep disturbance
  • Trembling
  • Muscle tension

More often than not, GAD co-exists with other mental disorders such as:

  • Major depression
  • Other anxiety disorders such as panic disorders
  • Substance use disorders
  • Eating disorders
