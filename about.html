Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

is much more than just feeling anxious. People with GAD find it difficult to control their worries, and this uncontrollable anxiety can interfere with day-to-day life.

GAD is one of the most common of mental disorders affecting approximately 6.8 million adults in the United States.

Symptoms of Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

The following table lists some of the key differences between normal anxiety and generalized anxiety disorder:

Normal Anxiety Generalized Anxiety Disorder
Worrying about a specific event such as
an exam, job interview, etc		 Constant, chronic, unsubstantiated worry
Able to set aside worries and focus on other things Feeling like your anxiety is uncontrollable,
and there's nothing that can be done to stop it
Anxiety and worrying for a short period of time Anxiety and worrying almost everyday
for at least six months
Normal, adaptive, and meant to improve people's
well-being and functioning		 Impairs people's well-being and functioning
and interferes with everyday life.

Physical symptoms of GAD may include:

  • Headaches
  • Nausea
  • Sleep disturbance
  • Trembling
  • Muscle tension

More often than not, GAD co-exists with other mental disorders such as:

  • Major depression
  • Other anxiety disorders such as panic disorders
  • Substance use disorders
  • Eating disorders
  • Body dysmorphic disorder
  • Obsessive-compulsive disorder

Treatment for GAD

Generalized Anxiety Disorder is treatable yet only 43.2% of those with GAD are receiving treatment. (ADAA)
If you are struggling with GAD, there are various treatments available that could help you. Talk with a healthcare professional to discuss the best treatment option for you.

Cognitive Behavior Therapy is said to be one of the most effective form of psychotherapy to treat GAD. Cognitive Behavior Therapy focuses on changing patterns of thinking that could be behind ones anxiety.

Learn more about Cognitive Behavioral Therapy here.

Medications including SSRIs, SNRIs, and antidepressants could be used in conjunction with therapy to treat anxiety disorders. It is generally safe, but there could be potentially dangerous drug interactions and side effects, so consult a doctor before use.

Learn more about medication options to treat GAD here.
